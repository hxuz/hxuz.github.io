Dropbox AI universal search preview

Two-week sprint, 6x-ed impressions, 2x-ed click through rate

Exploring patterns for organizally introducing AI features into everyday workflows

Launched in October 2024, Dropbox Dash features an AI-powered universal search for companies to find, organize, and protect documents and content.

This project focused on user acquisition by integrating an AI search preview into core search, leading existing Dropbox users to Dropbox Dash. I experimented multiple forms and interaction patterns to shape the direction of the AI preview, while navigating complex beta-stage technical constraints.

Challenge

To attract potential users, a strong first impression is critical. The design challenge centered on how to integrate AI search with keyword search: how to surface AI results, more prominent in a container or interwoven with traditional search? When and how to introduce Dropbox Dash? Each approach implied distinct design direction and strategic trade-offs.

Solution

Streamlined two-step setup to help users quickly access universal search results

To increase discoverability of the Dash limited preview, I designed the ‚Äúconnector‚Äù step that shows up before search results. This step allows users without any connected apps to easily add their favorite apps and explore Dropbox Dash.

Not only did this design enhance the user experience, but it also significantly boosted impressions and drove a notable increase in waitlist sign-ups.

Enhanced visual design to showcase the value of AI universal search

After the first MVP experiment tested different layouts for search results, I refreshed the visual design of the Dash limited preview section in the following sprint. The new design made the section more visually appealing and easier to digest.

By incorporating animated product assets, I implemented a Dash introduction modal that appears when users connect to a third-party app for the first time. This helped users quickly understand how the universal search works and what its capabilities are.

The updated visual design not only made the section more eye-catching but also improved the information hierarchy, clarity, and visual coherence on the search results page. The visual refresh delivered a sleek first impression, reinforcing Dash universal search as a valuable and premium feature.
