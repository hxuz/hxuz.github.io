I redefined two initiatives into one integrated effort, addressing the long-standing dark pattern, enhancing user experience, and ultimately boosting annual recurring revenue.

The dilemma

Upsell misleads users to change plans unintentionally

The current upsell during checkout encourages users to select the 3,000 GB free trial, but it doesn't clearly indicate that this switches them to a higher-cost plan once the trial ends. While this tactic boosts revenue for Dropbox, it negatively impacts user retention for the Dropbox Essentials plan and hinders the user experience.

Checkout is a high-risk surface that prioritizes revenue

Although dark patterns are something we want to avoid, removing a feature that generates significant revenue is challenging. The checkout surface is heavily revenue-driven, making it difficult to implement changes that could negatively affect business goals.
